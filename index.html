End-to-end coverage of the semiconductor value chain — from raw inputs (gases, wafers, photoresist, materials) through EDA software, chip designers, foundries, memory, analog/power, fab equipment, test, and OSAT/packaging, out to distribution and the end clients (Apple, the hyperscalers, automakers) whose capex pulls the whole chain. Use the chain-layer filter to isolate one tier at a time. Sources & method. Prices, market caps, and recent fiscal-year results gathered from public market data (~9–10 Jun 2026); ratios, ROIC, FCF quality, moat scores, and all forward figures are the author's illustrative estimates and normalizations, not consensus pulls. Japanese fiscal years generally end in March; the global names (ASML, TSMC, AMAT, LRCX, KLAC) report in USD on their own calendars. SBC is immaterial for the Japanese issuers but is more meaningful for the US names, so the SBC-adjusted basis moves their margins modestly. Reverse-DCF uses a transparent 10-year explicit FCF-to-equity model with a Gordon terminal value; per-share revenue, net cash, and starting FCF margins are approximations.

Figures are illustrative — verify live before acting. This is an analytical tool, not investment advice; it reflects no view on your objectives, and the author is not a financial adviser.
